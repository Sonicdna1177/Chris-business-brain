## SECTION 2: OFFER INVENTORY

### Instructional Tandem
- **What it is:** A tandem paragliding flight where the instructor handles launch and landing; the student is airborne with a qualified pilot the entire time
- **Who it's for:** First-time experience seekers; people deciding whether to progress to full instruction
- **What it delivers:** First experience of paragliding; connection to the craft before committing to a full course
- **How it works:** 1.5-hour session; the student flies but the instructor controls launch and landing
- **Duration/scope:** 1.5 hours
- **Price:** $299
- **Status:** Active

### Intro Day
- **What it is:** A full day of hands-on ground handling — the student learns to launch, control, and stop a paraglider on the ground
- **Who it's for:** Students beginning from zero; first step in the full certification pathway
- **What it delivers:** Foundational handling skills; the beginning of physical familiarity with the glider before first flight
- **How it works:** Full day on the ground with instructor supervision
- **Duration/scope:** Full day
- **Price:** $370
- **Status:** Active

### PG1
- **What it is:** Six short flights from a 50m hill plus ground school
- **Who it's for:** Students progressing from Intro Day toward full licence
- **What it delivers:** First real flights; introduction to parachute deployment, parachute landing falls, ingress/egress, radio and hand signals, logbook procedure, landing approaches
- **How it works:** Six managed short flights; classroom ground school sessions
- **Duration/scope:** [NEEDS MORE INFO: typical number of days for PG1 not specified]
- **Price:** $530
- **Status:** Active

### PG2
- **What it is:** 34 flights completing the student's IPPI international pilot licence
- **Who it's for:** Students who have completed PG1 and are ready for full licence progression
- **What it delivers:** Three-axis glider control (roll, pitch, yaw); descent techniques; advanced landing; advanced meteorology; decision-making processes and go/no-go judgement; IPPI licence on completion
- **How it works:** Average five flights per day; minimum nine days; structured progression beyond IPPI minimum requirements
- **Duration/scope:** 34 flights; minimum 9 days
- **Price:** $15 per flight ($510 minimum for 34 flights)
- **Status:** Active

### SIV Training
- **What it is:** Collapse recovery and advanced pilotage training — the highest level of paragliding instruction
- **Who it's for:** PG2-licensed pilots and those holding equivalent international qualifications — the programme is open to any pilot at that standard, not NZ licence holders exclusively
- **What it delivers:** Practical collapse recovery skills; advanced pilotage; SIV endorsement
- **How it works:** 3-day programme. Includes a pre-briefing video course, pre-briefs before each flight, in-flight training, post-flight debrief, and three flights per day (9 flights total across the programme)
- **Duration/scope:** 3 days · ~9 flights total
- **Price:** $2,400 NZD
- **Status:** Active (Chris is a qualified SIV Instructor)

## SECTION 3: HOW DELIVERY WORKS

1. **First contact:** Student finds Infinity via Google search or referral; reviews and regional exclusivity drive enquiry
2. **Entry point:** Instructional tandem or Intro Day — both are standalone; Intro Day is the first step in the certification pathway
3. **Intro Day:** Full day of ground handling — student learns to launch, control, and stop the glider on the ground. Hands-on from the first hour.
4. **PG1:** Six short flights from a 50m hill plus ground school covering safety fundamentals (parachute deployment, landing falls, ingress/egress, radio, logbook, landing approaches)
5. **PG2:** 34 flights at an average of five per day; students progress through three-axis control, descent techniques, advanced meteorology, and decision-making. Standard exceeds IPPI minimum throughout.
6. **Graduation:** Licence issued only when the student has demonstrated genuine pilot autonomy — not just flight count completion
7. **Post-licence:** SIV training available; one-to-one coaching programme in development

## SECTION 6B: COMPETITIVE RESEARCH FINDING (June 2026 — CORRECTED)

Initial research flagged Extreme Air (Coronet Peak) as an active competitor using "beyond minimum requirements" language similar to Infinity's positioning. This was incorrect — Extreme Air has been confirmed permanently closed for approximately five years (Trip.com lists the business as permanently closed). Cached website copy and review platforms had not reflected this. Extreme Air is not an active competitor and should be disregarded in positioning decisions.

Wings & Waves (Auckland) does use similar phrasing ("we teach well beyond these [minimums]") but operates outside Infinity's direct Queenstown/Otago market and is not a direct local competitor.

**Corrected finding:** Within Infinity's actual market, no active competitor was found explicitly claiming to exceed the minimum standard. "Beyond the minimum standard" remains usable language. The autonomy/judgement framing is still recommended as the primary positioning because it's more specific and descriptive of what Infinity actually does — not because the alternative phrase is unsafe to use.

**Lesson:** Always verify a competitor's current operating status before treating their website copy as an active competitive signal — closed businesses' pages and review listings can remain live and indexed long after the business has stopped trading.

## SECTION 9: COMMUNICATION GUIDELINES

**Tone by context:**
- LinkedIn posts: Direct, expert, aviation-authoritative; short sentences; specific details (flight counts, stages, techniques); no softening language
- Sales conversations: Plain and honest — state the standard clearly, name the commitment required, let the fit self-select
- Client onboarding: Responsibility-first; from day one students are told they are in charge of their own safety; high expectation framed as respect, not intimidation

**Language to use:**
autonomous pilot, go/no-go decision, conditions assessment, pilotage standard, aviation, IPPI, SIV, responsible for your own flying, judgement call, ground handling, three-axis control, flight standard, beyond the minimum, genuine capability

**Language to avoid:**
adventure experience, fun for everyone, beginner-friendly, at your own pace, exciting, unforgettable, bucket list, try paragliding, safe and easy, anyone can do it

**Hard limits:**
- Never compare paragliding to skiing or mountain biking — this directly contradicts the school's philosophy
- Never frame the licence as the end goal — autonomous pilot judgement is the end goal
- Never soften the seriousness of aviation to make it sound more accessible
