Personal Story Document
Client: Chris Connolly · June 2026
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

# PERSONAL STORY DOCUMENT
## Chris Connolly

---

## SECTION 1: ORIGIN & FOUNDATION

### The Moment That Changed Everything

When I first went to Poly Tech to learn automotive engineering, I learned how to learn. Up to that point, I had never been much of an academic. When I found something that I was deeply interested in, I learned at a rate that shocked me. I became the number one student in New Zealand in the first year of study.

### The Person Who Shaped Him

My mum. I remember her saying to me, "If a job's worth doing, it's worth doing right." Her quiet, stoic actions day by day and her kindness and empathy towards others is something that I saw and admired in her, and still do.

### The Value Running His Decisions

If you want something, go out and get it. We were taught from an early age that if we wanted something, all we had to do was work for it.

---

## SECTION 2: CHARACTER UNDER PRESSURE

### The Hardest Chapter

The hardest period of my life was around 18 to 23 years old, when I lost my best friend Tim and he died. I went through a very dark night of the soul, and after many years I came out the other side. It was very dark, very bleak, very lost, very alone. When I came out the other side, I knew I'd never go back there because I had found the bottom of the hole.

### The Risk That Defined Him

Buying out the paragliding school was definitely one of the scariest moments I had ever had. Going into financial debt was a real risk. The risk was that I would fail and lose everything and be in the hole. What happened on the other side of it? It was one of the best things I've ever done. I have become a more confident business person and investor as a result.

### The Mistake That Built Him

Failing at the Paragliding World Championships. That hurt badly. After four years of competing and training and competition flying to get selection for the New Zealand team, then go to the World Championships and to fly really badly in the World Championships really hurt me. It also wasn't what I thought it would be. It cost me a lot of time and a lot of money to go and train and chase after that goal, and it wasn't the outcome that I wanted it to be. It didn't give me the satisfaction that I wanted it to. The cost taught me that chasing after an extrinsic goal doesn't necessarily bring me happiness, and that I'm allowed to reset, regroup, and reimagine what I actually want in my life and to find out what makes me truly happy.

### Standing His Ground

When we split the paragliding business, I held a position of splitting the company in half and splitting the debt of the company in half, paying half of each. I believed that to be the right thing at the time and still do.

---

## SECTION 3: THE WORK & THE WHY

### When He Knew

After learning how to teach in Queenstown, when I went back to Wellington and started teaching on my own, I knew that I had come to a place where I could instruct by myself without any backup or safety guards in place.

### What He Does Differently

From day one, we place the entire emphasis on the pilot being 100% responsible for all of their own actions. They learn meteorology to a high level, and from that they can understand good judgement, and from good judgement they can make a clear decision of go or no-go.

### The Reframe That Changes Everything

Judgement calls and decision-making processes. Taking extreme ownership for everything that happens in paragliding gives you ultimate freedom. By taking responsibility for everything that happens, it frees you from the victim mentality and into empowerment. If you make a mistake, that's your fault. If everything goes great, that's your fault too. This allows you to learn from either the good or the bad and grow consistently with growth-based thinking rather than result-based thinking.

*Where it came from:* I learnt it in personal development; however, it crosses over to paragliding directly.

### The Work That Made Him Proud

A specific client is Grant, who had learned at a couple of other schools in Thailand and Spain. He came to us, went through our entire course, and became an autonomous pilot that was confident in flying and choosing his own pathway of flying. It also expanded his imagination of what was possible for himself. He went from a very timid pilot to a pilot who could fly anywhere in the world and does so.

*How that change is measured:* We see the change in becoming a pilot through them making their own decisions and then feeding back the consequences of their decisions to them.

---

## SECTION 4: WORLDVIEW & DIRECTION

### What Everyone Gets Wrong

New pilots not having extreme ownership over their own decisions. A lot of schools make a lot of the decisions for their students, and when those students then leave the school, they have a big gap between being able to make good judgments and decisions. Why does it keep happening? Because the schools take too much responsibility for their students.

### How Success Changed Shape

I used to think that success was determined by a financial amount, a profit and loss statement, and being a good business owner. Now I think success is understanding what makes me happy and keeping myself in a place that I am balanced in my mind and can see when I am imbalanced.

### Where This Is All Going

I think the industry is going to move more into digital learning, and that there'll be other pathways for creating better pilots rather than just going to school once and then struggling by yourself to learn. There'll be clear pathways for you to progress as a pilot and gain real-world skills and knowledge.

*On the limits of digital:* I think you can practise the methods of learning judgement, but decision making has to be made on the day, on the hill, before you go into the air.

### What People Actually Get

They are an autonomous pilot. They have the ability to go anywhere in the world and make really good choices about whether it's safe for them at their ability and skill level to go fly, and that creates longevity in the sport.
