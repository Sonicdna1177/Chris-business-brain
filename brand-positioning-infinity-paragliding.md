# BRAND POSITIONING
## Infinity Paragliding

---

## CATEGORY DEFINITION

You are not:
- An adventure tourism operator
- A try-paragliding experience
- A standard licence-delivery school

You operate as: The highest-standard paragliding education institution in New Zealand — where the measure of success is not a licence issued, but an autonomous pilot produced.

This shifts the category from: "Paragliding school"
To: "Serious aviation education for pilots who intend to fly for life"

---

## COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

**Who else is in this space:**
Other New Zealand paragliding schools; international schools where NZ students have previously trained (Thailand, Spain, Europe); online resources and general adventure sport schools.

**What they offer:**
Licence delivery, flight count programmes, adventure experiences, tourism-adjacent packages. Most market on ease of access, scenic location, or excitement rather than on the standard of instruction or the quality of pilot produced.

**The gap they leave open:**
Competitive research (June 2026) initially flagged Extreme Air (Coronet Peak) as using similar "beyond minimum requirements" language — but Extreme Air has been confirmed permanently closed for approximately five years (Trip.com lists the business as permanently closed; cached web copy and review listings had not been updated to reflect this). Extreme Air is not an active competitor and should be disregarded.

Wings & Waves (Auckland) does use similar language — "we teach well beyond these [minimums]" — but operates in a different city and region, and is not part of Infinity's direct competitive set in Queenstown/Otago.

Net finding: within Infinity's actual market (Queenstown/Otago), no active competitor was found making an explicit "beyond minimum standard" claim. The phrase remains usable. That said, the autonomy and judgement framing is still the stronger, more specific positioning regardless — it describes precisely what Infinity does (measuring success by decision-making capability, not licence or flight count) rather than a general claim of exceeding requirements, which is harder to substantiate distinctly even when no one else nearby is saying it. Combined with the proof stack — 1,000+ students and 133 reviews at 5.0 — Infinity has a defensible, evidence-backed position either way.

---

## STRATEGIC ANCHORS

**Frame of Reference:** Serious paragliding education

**Point of Difference (POD):**
Infinity holds every student to a standard of genuine pilot autonomy that goes beyond the IPPI minimum — covering decision-making, conditions assessment, and go/no-go judgement as the core curriculum outcome, not a bonus. No other school in the region makes this claim with the 13-year track record and SIV instructor qualification to back it up.

**Point of Parity (POP):**
IPPI-recognised licence delivery; qualified instructors; real flight experience; weather-dependent scheduling; ground school; equipment provision.

**Strategic Sacrifice:**
Infinity does not serve tourists who want to try paragliding once and call it a day. It does not serve people looking for the cheapest or fastest path to a licence. It does not soften its standards for students who are not willing to take responsibility for their flying. It does not position itself as accessible to everyone — because not everyone is the right fit, and being honest about that is what protects the school's integrity and the safety of its students.

**Competitive note (corrected):** Extreme Air, previously flagged as a direct competitor using similar language, has been confirmed permanently closed (~5 years). It is not an active competitor and the earlier caution against "beyond minimum standard" language on that basis no longer applies. Wings & Waves (Auckland) uses similar phrasing but is outside Infinity's direct Queenstown/Otago market. The autonomy/judgement framing remains the stronger positioning on its own merits — it's more specific and harder to imitate — but "beyond the minimum standard" is not currently claimed by any active competitor in Infinity's actual market.

## BRAND ARCHITECTURE

**Brand Promise:**
"Infinity Paragliding promises that every student who completes training will become a pilot capable of making independent, well-founded decisions about when, where, and whether to fly — and will have the skills and knowledge to back those decisions up."

**Brand Essence:** Serious flight. Genuine capability.

**The Name:** "Infinity" comes from a real SIV manoeuvre — the infinite tumble/spiral — but for Chris it carries a second, personal layer: it ties into the infinity of the universe, of life, of everything. The name operates on two levels at once, exactly like the brand itself: technically precise on the surface (a manoeuvre any serious pilot would recognise), and philosophically expansive underneath (a statement about the scale of what flying connects you to). This duality is worth using directly in founder storytelling, "why this name" content, and any moment where the brand needs to show depth beyond technical competence.

**Brand Archetype:** The Mentor / Sage
- Why: Chris's entire teaching philosophy is built around transferring genuine knowledge and responsibility to the student — not performing expertise, but creating competence. His credibility comes from what he knows and what his students become, not from how he presents himself.
- How it shows up: (1) Students are placed in the pilot's seat of responsibility from day one — this is a mentor's move, not a coach's. (2) The curriculum goes beyond what's required because Chris cares more about the pilot his student becomes than the metric that gets them certified.

**Recommended:** Option 7 — *The Pilot You Become.*
It captures Infinity's core positioning (pilot quality over licence status) in four words, works across all contexts, and sits at the exact intersection of the student's desire (identity transformation) and the school's differentiator (standard of who they produce). It also scales well as a digital school and high-ticket programme expand — the statement is as true for a $370 Intro Day as it is for a $25,000 one-to-one coaching intensive.

## STRATEGIC DO'S AND DON'TS

**DO:**
1. Lead every piece of content and communication with the pilot-autonomy outcome, not the licence credential
2. Speak to aviation's stakes honestly — this is what separates Infinity from every adventure tourism operator in the region
3. Use specific student transformation stories (Grant) as proof of the standard, not testimonials about how "great the experience was"
4. Name the decision-making curriculum explicitly — it's the differentiator no other school is marketing on
5. Hold the high-expectation positioning even when it excludes some prospective students — the sacrifice is the positioning

**DON'T:**
1. Frame Infinity as an adventure experience or tourism product — this collapses the entire positioning
2. Compete on price — the high standard is the reason the price is what it is
3. Serve students who are unwilling to take responsibility for their flying — they're not the fit and diluting for them weakens the brand
4. Use flight count or licence delivery as the headline success metric in any marketing context
5. Soften the language around aviation's risks — doing so is both dishonest and contradicts the school's entire philosophy

## THE STRATEGIC ENEMY

"Infinity Paragliding's true opponent is not another school or another instructor —
it is the global paragliding industry's habit of treating aviation like a sport, licensing pilots to a minimum standard, and measuring success by flight count rather than genuine pilot autonomy."

That enemy shows up in every school that rushes a student to their licence, in every industry publication that compares paragliding to skiing, and in every pilot who holds a piece of paper but not the judgement that should come with it. Infinity exists to produce the opposite of that outcome.

## POSITIONING NARRATIVE
*(For pitches, introductions, and partnerships)*

Infinity Paragliding exists because the global standard for paragliding instruction is too low — and the gap between holding a licence and being a genuinely capable, autonomous pilot is costing pilots their confidence, their longevity in the sport, and in some cases their lives.

Chris Connolly believes that paragliding is aviation, that aviation demands to be treated seriously, and that the highest service a paragliding school can provide is not a certificate but a pilot who can stand on any hill in any conditions and make the right call.

Unlike schools that teach to IPPI minimums and count flights, Infinity trains every student in decision-making, conditions assessment, three-axis pilotage, and autonomous go/no-go judgement — to a standard that goes far beyond what most schools attempt.

This makes Infinity the serious aviation education school for adults who want to fly for life — not just for a season.

Proven through students like Grant, who trained at schools in Thailand and Spain and came to Infinity specifically to finally be trained properly — and left 45 flights later as a pilot in his own right.
